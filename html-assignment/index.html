About Me

From dial-up to now

I remember dial-up internet with fondness. I was born in Ottawa and lived in a rural area with dial-up for nearly half my life. Loading a website with images took a minute or two and you needed LimeWire for music. Downloading videos was very time consuming. I eventually moved into the city, first Ottawa, then Montreal. For the latter half of my live I've been able to bask in very high-speed internet. Although I hated the 28.8 kbps speeds, I'm glad it gave me perspective.

My first internet experience

I didn't really get internet at first. Around 1996, give or take one or two years, I had my first experience. During the summer, my family went to Rhode Island to visit my aunt. She showed us the internet on her computer. I remember she told me that I can learn anything. She asked me, an eight-year-old, what I would like to know. I wasn't sure what I was getting into, I didn't really get it. Either way, I had recently seen a stick bug, so my mother suggested that. My aunt pulled up Microsoft® Encarta and searched for stick bug. There was so much information, I couldn't have read it all. My aunt offered to print it when I looked interested. I went home with a very thick, stapled document about stick bugs that I never looked at again. I'm glad I figured out the point of the internet eventually.
